Jawaban:

Weekend in Solo

In the last week me and my parents went to solo which was located in central java, indonesia. We drive by car. We were enjoyed the journey all day long Although it took two days to get there.We were fun, me and my sister always joke in the car. And my father told us the funny stories and his stories make us laugh out loud.

My first day in our village, i and my sister visited my siblings and friends. We are welcomed with happiness.When i look at my parents, they were cried because they were so happy meeting with their family. And next day our family go to jurug, jurug is a zoo that famous in solo, in jurug, had a many animal such as tiger, lion, bird, girraffe and another animal that we can not said.

In the evening, we were back home and did other private activities such as made an conversation, told the story and make a joke again. Time run so fast, because the next day was our last day in this village. So, we took a photo together to bring in our home. They were our family and always to be.

Jawaban:

a small village accompanied by dew and morning breeze greets every villager. The rainy season has arrived where farmers are busy preparing Lombok rice (ampar) crops. Early in the morning after breakfast the farmers flocked to visit the rice fields which had been plowed yesterday to plant small rice plants.

I was born in a small village in the district of East Lombok, like other children who were born in the village, we played very happily, away from the bustle of the metropolitan city. When we didn't go to school on Sundays, we would be busy in the fields helping our parents prepare seeds, land and so on.

In general, villagers, our daily life is colored by working in the rice fields and coming home late to rest, very different from in urban areas who are always busy with their own activities. Life in the village is very beautiful, health is guaranteed, the air is clean, the spring is still pure and the supply of natural foodstuffs.

Around school we usually go to play in the river, some take a bath, some build a small irrigation (reban) which deliberately collects the water and then opens it when it is flowing to friends' fields, although it is still on a small scale but it is enough to make lessons for us when we grow up.

Lombok is a producer of agricultural materials that still supply national needs. When the main harvest is in Sembalun, trucks carrying agricultural materials will be lined up to be taken out of the area, villages in Lombok are very fertile, one of which is Sembalun village which is still at the foot of Mount Rinjani.

The agricultural system in Lombok uses the subak system, or is chaired by (pekasih). Pekasih's function is the person or place we ask for agricultural water, pekasih is usually also chosen by farmers who are able to do justice, evenly by irrigation and other irrigation.

We must be proud to be born in a village, the village teaches us to be more active in working, to prioritize kinship, tolerance, cooperation and that is one of the things that distinguishes villages from cities in our beloved archipelago. the village is a teacher, the village is a role model, and the village is pure teaching.

The life of rural communities is slowly starting to undergo a transition, as the classical village era has begun to shift towards the cycle of small towns and begins to leave traces of the original village life, which usually children are told before bedtime tales.

That's the story of my hometown first, this story will continue to be told from generation to generation, although sooner or later it will be lost to the times.
