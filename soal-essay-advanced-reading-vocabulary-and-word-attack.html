17. 2 Make an outline for an essay about “Fast Food” which consists of 4 paragraphs (introduction, problems, problem solving, and conclusion)! Then write the essay in 150-200 words! Pay attention to the sentence structure and the selection of vocabulary items that are suitable for advanced level of writing! - READY REFERENSI WA O856-55OO-5OOO - READY REFERENSI WA O856-55OO-5OOO.


Outline:

I. Introduction

A. Definition and popularity of fast food

B. Thesis statement: While fast food is convenient and popular, it also poses several problems that need to be addressed.

II. Problems with Fast Food

A. Health concerns and obesity

B. Lack of nutritional value

C. Environmental impact

D. Negative effects on local economies

III. Problem Solving

A. Education and awareness campaigns

B. Government regulations and policies

C. Promotion of healthier fast food options

D. Support for local and sustainable food systems

IV. Conclusion

A. Recap of the problems with fast food

B. Importance of taking action to address these issues

C. Call to action for individuals, policymakers, and the fast food industry to work towards a healthier and more sustainable food culture.

Essay:

Fast Food: Addressing the Problems

Introduction:

Fast food has become a pervasive part of modern society, offering quick and convenient meals to millions of people around the world. However, while fast food has gained immense popularity, it also brings forth a range of problems that need to be recognized and addressed. In this essay, we will explore the problems associated with fast food and propose potential solutions.

Problems with Fast Food:

One of the major concerns with fast food is its impact on public health. The high levels of fat, sugar, and sodium in fast food contribute to a range of health issues, including obesity, diabetes, and cardiovascular diseases. Moreover, fast food often lacks essential nutrients, leading to a diet that is low in vitamins, minerals, and fiber.

Another problem is the environmental impact of fast food production. The intensive farming practices required to meet the high demand for ingredients contribute to deforestation, greenhouse gas emissions, and water pollution. Additionally, the excessive packaging and waste generated by fast food outlets contribute to the global waste problem.

Furthermore, the fast food industry's domination can have negative effects on local economies. Large fast food chains often displace local businesses, leading to job losses and a decline in community development. This shift towards centralized food systems also disconnects consumers from the source of their food and reduces support for local farmers.

Problem Solving:

Addressing the problems associated with fast food requires a multi-faceted approach. Education and awareness campaigns can help promote healthier food choices and inform the public about the consequences of excessive fast food consumption. Government regulations and policies are crucial in regulating food quality, labeling, and advertising to protect public health and the environment.

Promoting healthier options within the fast food industry can also make a significant impact. Encouraging the inclusion of nutritious menu items and reducing portion sizes can provide healthier alternatives to consumers. Additionally, supporting local and sustainable food systems can strengthen local economies, reduce environmental impacts, and provide fresher and healthier food options.

Conclusion:

Fast food may offer convenience and instant gratification, but it also presents a range of problems that cannot be ignored. The negative impact on public health, the environment, and local economies necessitates action. It is crucial for individuals to make informed food choices, policymakers to implement regulations, and the fast food industry to embrace healthier and more sustainable practices. By collectively working towards a healthier and more sustainable food culture, we can mitigate the problems associated with fast food and pave the way for a better future.

5. According to Brown (2004: 187-188), he devided micro and macro skill of reading as stated below:

1) Microskills

a) Discriminate among distinctive graphemes and orthographic patterns of English.

b) Retain chunks of language of different lengths in short term memory.

c) Process writing at an efficient rate of speed to suit the purpose.

d) Recognize a core of words, and interpret word order patterns and their significance.

e) Recognize grammatical word classes (nouns, verb etc.) systems (e.g. tense, agreement, pluralisation), patterns, rules, and elliptical forms.

f) Recognize that a particular meaning may be expressed in different grammatical forms.

g) Recognize cohesive devices in written discourse and their role in signaling the relationship between and among clauses.

2) Macroskills

a) Recognize the rhetorical forms of written discourse and their significance for interpretation.

b) Recognize the communicative functions of written texts, according to form and purpose.

c) Infer context that is not explicit by using background knowledge.

d) From described events, ideas, etc. Infer links and connections between events, deduce causes and effects, and detect such relations as main idea, supporting idea, new information, given information, generalization, and exemplification.

e) Distinguish between literal and implied meanings.

f) Detect culturally specific references and interpret them in a context of the appropriate cultural schemata.

g) Develop and use a battery of reading strategies such as scanning and skimming, detecting discourse markers, guessing the meaning of words from context, and activating schemata for the interpretation of texts.

6. Skimming - Reading rapidly for the main points

Scanning - Reading rapidly through a text to find specific information required

Extensive - Reading longer texts, often for pleasure and for an overall understanding

Intensive - Reading shorter texts for detailed information with emphasis on precise understanding

These different types of skills are used quite naturally when reading in a mother tongue.

7. Top-down processing of language happens when someone uses background information to predict the meaning of language they are going to listen to or read. Rather than relying first on the actual words or sounds (bottom up), they develop expectations about what they will hear or read, and confirm or reject these as they listen or read. Top-down processing is thought to be an effective way of processing language; it makes the most of what the person brings to the situation.

Soal Reading:

Baca paragraf di bawah ini, lalu jawablah pertanyaan-pertanyaan berikut.

"Adrian belajar sangat keras untuk ujian akhir minggu ini. Ia menghabiskan sebagian besar waktu luangnya untuk membaca buku dan mencatat poin yang penting. Kakaknya, Karina, mengingatkan Adrian untuk selalu mengerjakan latihan dan menjaga kesehatan dengan makan dengan baik serta beristirahat yang cukup."

Soal Direct & Indirect Commands:

A. Ubahlah kalimat-kalimat direct command di bawah ini menjadi indirect command:

1. "Tolong buka pintu itu," kata Rina.

1. Mia meminta Tomy untuk tidak bermain di luar saat hujan.
